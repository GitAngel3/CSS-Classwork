Kitten Care- Feeding, Training & Vaccination Tips

    If you’ve just welcomed a kitten into your home, you’re probably wondering where to start and how to take care of a kitten the right way. The early months are full of discovery, both for you and your little feline, as you learn to understand their needs and quirks. It can feel exciting, heartwarming, and yes, a little overwhelming at times. Kittens are naturally curious, playful, and bursting with energy, but they also need proper guidance and care to grow into healthy, well-adjusted adult cats. From figuring out what to feed them, to teaching them good habits, to keeping up with their vaccination schedule, there’s a lot to cover. In this blog, we’ll walk you through the three key pillars of kitten care: nutrition, training, and vaccinations. With the right approach, you’ll not only help your kitten thrive but also build a strong, loving bond that lasts a lifetime.

    • Siamese Cat Profile: Behaviour, Nutrition & Training Needs

    The Siamese cat has ancient roots in Thailand, where they were considered sacred and often kept by royalty. Their striking appearance is impossible to miss - slim bodies, long legs, large ears, and pointed colour patterns in shades like seal, lilac, chocolate, and blue.Over time, the breed has also given rise to variations, including the charming Snowshoe Siamese, a mix known for its white “boots” and playful temperament.When it comes to personality, Siamese cat behaviour is unlike any other. These cats are known for their chatty nature - their meows are often loud, expressive, and almost conversation-like.Highly social and people-oriented, they form deep bonds with their families. Their personality is affectionate, playful, and at times, demanding. Siamese cats dislike being left alone for a long time, so they’re best suited for homes where someone is usually around. With their intelligence and curiosity, they’re happiest when mentally and physically stimulated.
